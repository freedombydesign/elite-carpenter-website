Flooring Installation for Burlington Homes

Burlington's housing stock is genuinely varied. South Burlington's post-WWII detached homes from the 1950s-1970s often have original hardwood that needs refinishing or outdated carpet and linoleum that needs replacement. Newer suburbs have more recent flooring in various states of wear.

Lake Ontario's proximity means humidity is a consideration for flooring material selection. Waterproof options like luxury vinyl plank perform well in Burlington's climate, while hardwood requires proper installation and maintenance.

Elite Carpenter Home Renovation handles flooring installation for Burlington homes. We work with LVP, hardwood, engineered hardwood, tile, and carpet — and we prepare subfloors properly to ensure long-lasting results.

Flooring Considerations for Burlington Homes

Lake Ontario humidity: Burlington's lakefront location means higher humidity. We select materials and underlayment that handle moisture well, especially for basements and kitchens.

Post-WWII subfloors: South Burlington's older homes may have subfloors that need leveling or repair before new flooring installation. We assess and prepare properly.

Varied home ages: We tailor our approach based on whether your Burlington home has original substructure that needs special attention or newer construction.

Transitions: Burlington homes often have multiple flooring types meeting at doorways. We create clean, professional transitions.

Flooring Options We Install

  • Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP): Waterproof, durable, looks like hardwood. Ideal for kitchens, bathrooms, and basements. Typical cost: $3-$7 per sq ft installed.
  • Hardwood: Classic solid hardwood in oak, maple, or other species. Can be refinished multiple times. Best for main living areas.
  • Engineered Hardwood: More stable than solid hardwood, resists moisture better. Good for basements and kitchens.
  • Tile: Porcelain or ceramic tile for bathrooms, entryways, and kitchens. Extremely durable and waterproof.
  • Carpet: Comfortable and warm for bedrooms and basements. Wide range of styles and budgets.

Popular Flooring Choices for Burlington Homes

Wide-Plank LVP

Wide-plank luxury vinyl in natural wood tones offers the look of hardwood with waterproof performance — perfect for Burlington's climate.

Refinished Hardwood

South Burlington's original hardwood floors can often be sanded and refinished rather than replaced, saving cost while preserving character.

Large Format Tile

12x24 or larger porcelain tile in entryways and bathrooms provides a clean, modern look with minimal grout maintenance.

Basement LVP

Waterproof LVP in basements handles humidity while providing a warm, finished appearance over DRIcore subfloor.

How Much Does Flooring Installation Cost in Burlington?

Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) typically costs $3-$7 per square foot installed, including materials, underlayment, and labor. Hardwood and tile vary based on material selection. We provide detailed quotes after measuring your space.

How Long Does Flooring Installation Take?

Flooring installation timelines depend on the size of the area and type of flooring. A typical bedroom or kitchen takes 1-3 days. Full main-floor installations take longer, especially if subfloor preparation or repairs are needed.
