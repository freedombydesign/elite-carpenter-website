How Much Does a Kitchen Renovation Cost in Hamilton?

Kitchen renovation costs vary widely based on kitchen size, cabinet choice, countertop material, and whether you're doing a cosmetic refresh or full gut renovation with layout changes.

Budget Ranges

Cosmetic refresh (new cabinets, countertops, backsplash): Budget for stock cabinets and laminate or quartz countertops.

Full renovation (layout changes, moving plumbing/electrical): Higher budget for custom cabinets, granite/quartz, and structural work.

High-end renovation (custom cabinets, stone counters, appliances): Premium finishes and materials.

What's Included in a Kitchen Renovation

Cabinets

Cabinets are typically the largest expense in a kitchen renovation. Your options:

  • Stock cabinets: Pre-made sizes, limited styles, ready in 1-2 weeks
  • Semi-custom cabinets: More size options and styles, 4-6 week lead time
  • Custom cabinets: Built to your exact specs, premium materials, 8-12 weeks

Countertops

Countertop pricing varies by material:

  • Laminate: Budget-friendly, many colors, less durable
  • Quartz: Most popular choice, durable, low-maintenance, non-porous
  • Granite: Natural stone, requires sealing, classic look
  • Butcher block: Warm wood look, requires maintenance

Backsplash

  • Subway tile (most common)
  • Mosaic or patterned tile
  • Slab backsplash (quartz or stone)
  • Tile installation labor and materials

Flooring

  • Luxury vinyl plank (waterproof, durable)
  • Tile (porcelain or ceramic)
  • Engineered hardwood

Electrical and Lighting

  • Pot lights (recessed lighting)
  • Under-cabinet lighting
  • Pendant lights over island
  • Outlets (including GFI outlets near sink)
  • Panel upgrade if needed for electric range/oven

Plumbing

  • Sink and faucet
  • Dishwasher hookup
  • Moving plumbing for sink or island (if layout changes)

Labor

  • Demo (removing old cabinets, countertops, flooring)
  • Cabinet installation
  • Countertop templating and installation
  • Tile installation (backsplash and floor)
  • Electrical rough-in and final
  • Plumbing installation
  • Drywall repair and painting

Factors That Increase Cost

1. Layout Changes

Moving the sink or stove to a different location requires rerouting plumbing and gas lines or electrical, which adds significant cost. Keeping the same layout reduces costs.

2. Custom Cabinets

Custom cabinets cost significantly more than stock, but they maximize storage and fit odd dimensions perfectly.

3. High-End Countertops

Premium materials like quartz with waterfall edges, exotic granite, or marble cost more than standard quartz or laminate.

4. Kitchen Size

Larger kitchens mean more cabinets, more countertop square footage, more flooring, and more electrical/plumbing work.

5. Structural Changes

Removing walls to create open-concept layouts requires structural engineering, beam installation, and permits.

6. Heritage Homes

Older homes in Dundas, Ancaster, or Hamilton's heritage neighborhoods may have outdated wiring, plumbing, or structural issues that increase renovation complexity.

Ways to Save Money

  • Keep the same layout: Don't move the sink or stove
  • Choose stock cabinets: Faster and more affordable than custom
  • Limit backsplash area: Tile only behind the stove and sink instead of full walls
  • Standard countertop edge: Skip waterfall edges or fancy profiles
  • Refinish existing floors: If you have hardwood, refinish instead of replacing

Installation-Only Kitchen Renovations

Some homeowners prefer to source their own cabinets and countertops from big box stores (Home Depot, Ikea, Lowes) and hire a contractor just for installation. This can save money but requires you to handle ordering, delivery coordination, and troubleshooting if materials are wrong or delayed.

Timeline for Kitchen Renovations

Most kitchen renovations in Hamilton take 3-8 weeks:

  • Cosmetic refresh: 2-3 weeks (new cabinets, counters, backsplash)
  • Full renovation with layout changes: 6-8 weeks
  • Custom cabinets: Add 6-8 weeks for cabinet manufacturing before construction starts
