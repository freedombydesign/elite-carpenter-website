Kitchen Remodeling for Dundas Heritage Homes

Downtown Dundas, Pleasant Valley, and University Gardens are filled with 1920s-1940s character homes. Beautiful original millwork, high ceilings, and classic architecture — but kitchens from that era weren't designed for how we live today.

Small galley layouts, outdated plumbing, knob-and-tube wiring, and inefficient use of space are common challenges. Many Dundas homeowners want modern function while preserving the character of their heritage property.

Elite Carpenter Home Renovation specializes in kitchen renovations for older Dundas homes. We handle everything from layout redesign to electrical panel upgrades, custom cabinetry to countertop installation — coordinating all trades so you get a complete kitchen transformation.

Renovating Kitchens in Dundas Heritage Homes

Plaster walls: Original plaster walls require careful handling. We work around them or replace with drywall where needed, maintaining structural integrity.

Electrical upgrades: Many Dundas homes still have knob-and-tube wiring or undersized panels. Modern kitchens need dedicated circuits for appliances. We coordinate licensed electricians for panel upgrades and rewiring.

Plumbing considerations: Original cast iron or galvanized plumbing may need replacement. Relocating sinks or adding dishwashers requires careful planning in older homes.

Heritage approvals: If your property is heritage-designated or in a heritage district, exterior changes may require approval. We navigate the process.

Popular Kitchen Layouts for Dundas Homes

Galley to L-Shape

Opening up a cramped galley kitchen into an L-shape layout by removing a non-structural wall creates more counter space and flow.

Peninsula Addition

Adding a peninsula provides extra prep space, seating, and a natural division between kitchen and dining areas without full walls.

Heritage-Modern Blend

Shaker-style cabinets, classic subway tile, and traditional hardware paired with modern appliances and quartz countertops.

Pantry Integration

Older homes often lack pantry space. Floor-to-ceiling cabinets or a dedicated pantry closet maximize storage in compact kitchens.

How Long Does a Kitchen Renovation Take?

A complete kitchen renovation in a Dundas heritage home typically takes 6-10 weeks from demolition to final walkthrough. Timeline depends on scope — simple cabinet and countertop replacements are faster, while full layout changes with electrical and plumbing upgrades take longer.

We coordinate all trades (electricians, plumbers, tile setters, cabinet installers) so you're not managing multiple contractors. Heritage approvals or permit reviews can add time to the schedule.

Permits for Dundas Kitchen Renovations

Kitchen renovations typically require permits for electrical work, plumbing changes, and structural modifications. If your Dundas home is heritage-designated, you may also need heritage approval for certain changes.

We handle permit applications and coordinate inspections to ensure all work meets Ontario Building Code and local requirements.
