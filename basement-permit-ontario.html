Do You Need a Permit for Basement Renovation in Ontario?

Understanding Ontario building permit requirements for basement finishing, electrical, plumbing, and structural work.

The Short Answer

In Ontario, you typically need a building permit for basement renovation work that involves electrical, plumbing, structural changes, or adding a bedroom. Cosmetic updates like painting or flooring replacement usually don't require a permit.

When You ALWAYS Need a Permit

Electrical work: Adding circuits, outlets, or pot lights requires an ESA permit and inspection.

Plumbing: Installing or moving fixtures (toilet, sink, shower) requires a plumbing permit.

Structural changes: Moving or cutting into load-bearing walls, beams, or joists.

Adding a bedroom: Bedrooms require egress windows meeting Ontario Building Code minimum sizes.

Framing new walls: Most municipalities require permits for new framed walls in basements.

Work That Usually Doesn't Need a Permit

  • Painting walls and ceilings
  • Installing flooring (vinyl plank, carpet, laminate)
  • Replacing existing light fixtures (same location, no new wiring)
  • Installing trim and baseboards
  • Cosmetic updates that don't involve systems or structure

Why Permits Matter

Building permits ensure work meets Ontario Building Code safety standards. Unpermitted work can cause problems when you sell your home — buyers' home inspectors will flag unpermitted basements, and mortgage lenders may refuse financing until the work is either permitted retroactively or removed.

Insurance companies may also deny claims related to unpermitted work.

Egress Window Requirements

If you're adding a bedroom in your basement, Ontario Building Code requires an egress window for emergency escape. The window must have:

  • Minimum 0.35 m² (3.77 sq ft) unobstructed opening
  • No dimension less than 380 mm (15 inches)
  • Sill height no more than 1.5 m (59 inches) above the bedroom floor
  • Window well if below grade, with proper drainage

The Permit Process in Hamilton and Surrounding Areas

Most municipalities in the Hamilton area (including Dundas, Burlington, Ancaster, and Stoney Creek) follow similar permit processes:

  • Submit application: Include drawings showing the basement layout, electrical plan, and structural details
  • Plan review: Municipality reviews for code compliance (typically 2-3 weeks)
  • Pay permit fees: Fees vary by municipality and project scope
  • Construction: Work begins after permit is issued
  • Inspections: Rough-in inspections (framing, electrical, plumbing) and final inspection
