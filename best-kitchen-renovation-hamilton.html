Tips for Choosing a Kitchen Contractor in Hamilton

  • Get at least 3 quotes - Prices vary significantly between contractors
  • Check credentials - Ensure they have proper licensing and insurance for Ontario
  • Ask about permits - A good contractor handles permit applications
  • Review their portfolio - Ask to see completed projects similar to yours
  • Check references - Talk to past clients about their experience
  • Get everything in writing - Detailed contract with scope, timeline, and payment schedule
  • Understand the warranty - Know what's covered after completion

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a kitchen renovation cost in Hamilton?

Kitchen renovations in Hamilton typically range from $15,000 to $50,000+. A basic refresh (paint, hardware, countertops) runs $15,000-$25,000. A mid-range remodel with new cabinets and layout changes costs $25,000-$40,000. High-end custom kitchens can exceed $50,000. Factors affecting cost include kitchen size, cabinet quality, countertop material, appliances, and whether you're changing the layout.

How long does a kitchen renovation take?

Most kitchen renovations in Hamilton take 4-8 weeks from demolition to completion. A simple refresh might take 2-3 weeks. A full gut renovation with layout changes typically takes 6-10 weeks. Add 2-4 weeks for design and permit approval before construction begins.

Do I need permits for a kitchen renovation in Ontario?

You typically need permits if you're doing electrical work, plumbing changes, structural modifications, or moving walls. Cosmetic updates like painting, new cabinets (in same location), and countertop replacement usually don't require permits. A licensed contractor will advise you and handle permit applications.

What should I look for in a kitchen renovation contractor?

Look for: valid Ontario licenses and insurance, strong online reviews (Google, HomeStars), a portfolio of completed projects, clear communication, detailed written quotes, and willingness to pull permits. Red flags include asking for large deposits upfront, no written contract, or reluctance to provide references.

Can I live in my home during a kitchen renovation?

Yes, most homeowners stay in their home during kitchen renovations. Set up a temporary kitchen area with a microwave, toaster oven, and coffee maker in another room. Expect dust and noise during work hours. A full gut renovation may be more disruptive than a simple update.
